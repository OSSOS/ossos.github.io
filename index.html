{% include section-header.html title="Mysteries beyond Neptune" %}

The outer Solar System remains relatively unexplored. What lies beyond the orbit of Neptune? How many icy worlds are out there? Are they orbiting where they formed, or have their orbits changed? If they were scattered to where we see them today, how and when did this happen? OSSOS aims to help answer these - and many other questions.
To do this, we're discovering a large number of trans-Neptunian objects, with sophisticated techniques that allow us to measure and remove a number of biases introduced by the act of surveying.
